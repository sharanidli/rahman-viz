Data Sources

Track and film metadata was scraped from Wikipedia's discography pages for AR Rahman. This includes album names, track listings, singer credits, lyricist credits, director, cast, release year, and language.

Audio features come from Spotify's audio analysis, accessed via ReccoBeats. These include danceability, energy, valence, acousticness, liveness, and tempo for each track that has a Spotify match.

The dataset currently covers 168 albums, 2,100 tracks, and audio features for about 42% of those tracks. Coverage varies by album — the Data Quality page shows completeness for each one.

Audio Features Glossary

These features come from Spotify's audio analysis API. Each is a number between 0 and 1 (except tempo, which is in BPM).

  • Danceability — How suitable a track is for dancing. Combines tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength, and regularity. Higher = more danceable.
  • Energy — Perceptual intensity and activity. Energetic tracks feel fast, loud, and noisy. A Bach prelude scores low; a metal track scores high.
  • Valence — Musical positivity. High valence = happy, cheerful, euphoric. Low valence = sad, depressed, angry.
  • Acousticness — Confidence that a track is acoustic (no electronic amplification). 1.0 = high confidence the track is acoustic.
  • Liveness — Detects the presence of a live audience. Higher values mean greater likelihood the track was performed live.
  • Tempo (BPM) — The estimated tempo in beats per minute. Rahman's tracks range from 46 to 217 BPM, with a median around 115.

Limitations

  • Not every album has audio features. Spotify matching depends on track availability, which varies by region and rights. About 58% of tracks are missing audio data.
  • Wikipedia credits may be incomplete. Singer and lyricist credits on Wikipedia are community-maintained and may have gaps, especially for older or lesser-known albums.
  • Language data has noise. The language column in track CSVs sometimes contains Wikipedia section headers instead of actual languages. We filter these out where possible.
  • Audio features are algorithmic. Spotify's analysis is automated, not human-curated. Valence in particular can feel subjective — a raga-based track may score low on "happiness" while being deeply uplifting in its cultural context.
